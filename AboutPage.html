The Yale School of Art is a graduate school that confers Master of Fine Arts Degrees in Graphic Design, Painting/Printmaking, Photography, and Sculpture.

Artists and designers of unusual promise and strong motivation are provided an educational context in which they can explore the potential of their own talents in the midst of an intense critical dialogue. This dialogue is generated by their peers, by distinguished visitors, and by a faculty comprised of professional artists of acknowledged accomplishment.

The graduate student's primary educational experience at Yale is centered on the student's own studio activity. Supporting this enterprise are the experience, knowledge, and skills gained from rigorous, structured courses such as drawing, filmmaking, the relativity of color, and the rich academic offerings found throughout Yale.

The School of Art offers an undergraduate art major for students in Yale College. Students from other departments may enroll in elective courses with permission.

Degree

The Master of Fine Arts is the only degree offered by the School of Art, awarded upon successful completion of all course requirements and a faculty-approved thesis presentation.

The minimum residence requirement is two years, with work reviewed by faculty each term. Unsatisfactory performance may lead to dismissal from the program.

Course work carries a minimum of sixty credits, distributed based on the student's area of study in consultation with their faculty adviser.

Exhibits

Yale's art exhibitions feature exceptional student work, faculty projects, and visiting artist showcases, allowing students to participate in a professional gallery experience.

The School regularly curates public and private exhibitions to support artistic growth, critique, and campus-wide engagement.

Student exhibits help develop professional presentation skills, curatorial awareness, and artistic confidence within a supportive community.
